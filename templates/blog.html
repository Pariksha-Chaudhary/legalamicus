Evolution of Article 21 Jurisprudence

Constitutional Law | Advocate Aryan Sharma

Article 21 has evolved from a narrow procedural guarantee into a substantive source of human rights protection. The Supreme Court transformed its interpretation to include fairness, dignity, and reasonableness within the concept of life and liberty.

The expansion includes rights to privacy, livelihood, clean environment, and speedy trial, reflecting transformative constitutionalism.

Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India, AIR 1978 SC 597

Basic Structure Doctrine and Constitutional Supremacy

Constitutional Theory | Advocate Aryan Sharma

The Basic Structure doctrine ensures that Parliament cannot alter the core identity of the Constitution. Judicial review, secularism, and rule of law form part of this inviolable structure.

This doctrine preserves constitutional integrity against majoritarian excess.

Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala, (1973) 4 SCC 225

Freedom of Speech under Article 19(1)(a)

Fundamental Rights | Advocate Aryan Sharma

Freedom of speech forms the bedrock of democratic governance. However, reasonable restrictions under Article 19(2) permit regulation in the interest of sovereignty and public order.

Balancing liberty with responsibility remains central to constitutional adjudication.

Shreya Singhal v. Union of India, (2015) 5 SCC 1

Judicial Review under Articles 32 and 226

Writ Jurisdiction | Advocate Aryan Sharma

Judicial review enables constitutional courts to examine legislative and executive actions. It remains a basic feature of the Constitution.

Writ remedies such as Habeas Corpus and Mandamus protect individual rights.

L. Chandra Kumar v. Union of India, (1997) 3 SCC 261

Bail Jurisprudence and Personal Liberty

Criminal Law | Advocate Aryan Sharma

The principle that bail is the rule and jail the exception safeguards personal liberty. Courts assess risk of absconding and gravity of offence before denying bail.

State of Rajasthan v. Balchand, AIR 1977 SC 2447

Right to Privacy as a Fundamental Right

Human Rights | Advocate Aryan Sharma

The Supreme Court unanimously declared privacy intrinsic to Article 21. Informational autonomy and dignity now receive constitutional protection.

Justice K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India, (2017) 10 SCC 1

Public Interest Litigation and Social Justice

Access to Justice | Advocate Aryan Sharma

PIL democratized access to constitutional remedies, allowing courts to intervene in matters affecting marginalized communities.

S.P. Gupta v. Union of India, 1981 Supp SCC 87

Doctrine of Proportionality in Constitutional Law

Judicial Standards | Advocate Aryan Sharma

The doctrine of proportionality ensures that restrictions on rights are necessary and minimally invasive.

Modern Dental College v. State of MP, (2016) 7 SCC 353

Secularism as a Basic Feature

Constitutional Values | Advocate Aryan Sharma

Secularism forms part of the basic structure. The State must maintain principled distance from religious institutions.

S.R. Bommai v. Union of India, (1994) 3 SCC 1

Separation of Powers and Judicial Independence

Constitutional Governance | Advocate Aryan Sharma

Judicial independence safeguards rule of law and democratic stability. The Constitution carefully balances powers among three organs of State.

Supreme Court Advocates-on-Record Assn. v. Union of India, (1993) 4 SCC 441
